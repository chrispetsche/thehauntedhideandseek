Project description

As 1 of 2 six week sprints during a prototyping class in grad school, this multiplayer online VR experience for the Oculus Rift DK1 was a collaborative project that used the new technology to enhance the way players get to re-live their childhood memories from whereever they are as if they were back in their old neighnorhoods running around together.

With a night time setting at a creepy old farm house, no more than 3 hiders armed with a flashlight and the hope of staying hidden start out in a random room within the house and attempt to get the safe spot located out in the yard that's only accessible through the front door.

image above of hider about to be tagged out from behind by the sneaky seeker

The seekers on the other hand, who gets to play the role of a ghost, starts in a random positio outside the house. And since there can only be one seeker at a time, they have the creepy but funnest abilities in the game, using the televisions scattered about to teleport from room to rooms and scare the crap out of the hiders attempting to escape undetected.

System Design and Development

Tag System

Naturally there can't be a game of hide and seek if there is no way of knowing what happens if someone gets tagged. Since the entire experience is unique, so why not the tag system?! And it designed and coded in C# to works like this...

Each player starts out with a tag token, if you will, except the seeker who has none. They carry these tokens until the seeker creeps up close enough to reach out and touch their target. As soon as they are tagged out, the hidder is teleported to a random room and the token is left where they were tagged.


In order for the seeker to count the tag, they MUST pick up the token!!

image above of tag tokens and the players assigned colors
image above of seeker ghost hit by the glare of the flashlight
prior to it dropping its tag token

The tagged hider does have a chance to gain back and even earn more tokens themselves. As a part of their defense/ handicap they are equipped with a flashlight, as mentioned. Even though the light can give away the hiders position in the dark house, the seeker is vulnerable to its glare.


Should they get caught in the light, the system treats them as if they are a hider by teleporting them to a random respawn and a token is dropped. Like with seekers for token to count it MUST be picked up. But... hiders also have to reach the safe spot for it to lock in as a returned tag.

Now, if a hider is tagged without having a token the player is removed from the game until the round is over. If they still have a token when they reach the safe spot they are awarded another token. If they are carrying their token and stolen tokens, then they are awarded 1 token for their original plus 1 token for each one stolen.


The player with the least amount of tag tokens is automatically selected to be seeker in the next round. A round ends when time runs out or when the seeker has removed ALL hiders from the game. Rounds can vary in time.

Seeker Teleport System

The seeker was designed to move with a gimp to create a handicap that gave the hiders an advantage. As a slight counter to that, I designed a simple teleportation system to help equal things up some.

Inspired by creepy movies like "The Ring", it was settled that linking TVs throughout the house would be a fun way of going about this. I selected appropriate rooms to place the TVs in and coded them so that each one would teleport the seeker to a specific location the seeker would have to figure out as these locations changed every time a new game was loaded.

UI Design and Development

The UI for this project was relatively easy to set up. It uses Unitys old NGUI system to create world space canvases that 2D sprites can be attached to. A collider system, using stationary cubes where the buttons are located and a single cube moved about by the XBox controller, allows players to interact with the elements and make their selections. There are 3 pages the player can select from:

image above of ui main menu

Title Page:

Contains buttons leading the player to the game, credits and controls.

* If the player presses 'Play', they are given the optioin of four different characters. One of three hiders or the seeker. Once each slot is selected the game is initialized.

Credits Page:

Displays the efforts and contributions of all the team members. Also has a button sequence matching the main menu.

image above of ui controls page

Controls Page:

Provides the player with a quick view of the XBox controls that the game is mapped to, and again buttons leading to the other two menus.

Project status and availability

Status

The outcome of the project met every intended expectation. As an experiment with the early VR headset, it showed that in many cases simple was the better way to go. Not just with the limited amount of time given to complete the project, but extreme measures weren't needed to have a fun, thrilling and memorable experience.
